History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year.

The Beginning (1950s)

The first computerized game (that did not have physics) came out in the early 1950s. These included a game similar to tic-tac-toe called Bertie the Brain (1950) and a chess game by the famous Allen Turing called Turochamp (1952).In 1958, the physics game "Tennis for Two" came out which was a predecessor to the game Pong.

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

This time period showed the rise of home entertainment. The Magnavox Odyssey was one of the first attempts at a game concole, but it never caught on completly. In 1975, Atari came out with the Home Pong system because of the rise in popularity of their arcade game Pong.In 1983, the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) came out and became very popular.

North American Video Game Crash (1983)

In 1983, some of the consoles that were out where the Atari 2600, the NES, and the Sega Master System. During this time period, Video games were starting to lose popularity and home consoles were not making as much money. Many lower quality video games came out at this time, most notably the E.T. game.

The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

This time period showed the start to the first console wars. In 1989, Sega released the Genesis as a successor to the Master System. It was a big step up from Nintendo's NES and had 16-bit which was a competitive improvement. To battle this, Nintendo then released the Super NES console that also had 16-bit. The Genesis helped put Sega ahead of Nintendo with its better price point and bigger game library.

Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

During this time period, tech companies decided to throw their hat into the video game ring. On December 3, 1994, the Sony Playstation came out. It was then released in North America on September 9, 1995. In 2001, Microsoft released the Xbox console. It was a huge hit and it was the first american video game console since the Atari Jaguar. Even Apple tried their hand at gaming with the infamous Apple Bandai Pippin. It was very unsucsessfull.

Today in Gaming (2010-Now)

Today, gaming is a part of the daily lives for millions of people all around the world. Gaming has become a way people can make a living. It is so popular that streamers and e-sports players can earn thousands, if not millions of dollars playing them. It has also become easier to get into games with the rise of smartphones and tablets and the hundreds of free-to-play games. There are games everwhere and there is a game for everyone.
